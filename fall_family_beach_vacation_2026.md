# Fall 2026 beach vacation brainstorm for 3 families

Prepared: 2026-06-16

## Group and constraints

- Families: 6 adults, 6 kids aged 7, 7, 7, 6, 3, 1, and 0.
- Practical flight count: 10 paid seats if both babies travel as lap infants, plus infant fees. If either baby needs a seat, budget one more child fare.
- Starting point: Zurich preferred. Basel and Milan Malpensa are backup airports if fares are much better.
- Desired trip: warm, beach, swimmable sea or strong pool/resort setup.
- Duration: 10-15 days during Zurich school autumn holidays.
- Zurich 2026 autumn school holiday window: 2026-10-03 to 2026-10-18, based on EDK/IDES canton data. Note: Zurich canton data outside start of school year and Christmas are recommendations; municipalities can vary.

## Best date windows to price first

Price the same destination across several nearby windows before deciding. For school-holiday flights, moving away from Saturday/Sunday often matters more than choosing a cheaper airport.

- 2026-10-04 to 2026-10-16: 12 nights, avoids first Saturday outbound and last Sunday return.
- 2026-10-05 to 2026-10-17: 12 nights, good balance if Monday flights exist.
- 2026-10-06 to 2026-10-18: 12 nights, may save outbound but return Sunday can be expensive.
- 2026-10-07 to 2026-10-17: 10 nights, useful if hotel/flight prices spike.
- 2026-10-03 to 2026-10-17: 14 nights, convenient but likely peak pricing.

## Short answer

Best overall shortlist:

1. Antalya / Belek / Side, Turkey: best resort value, short direct flight, lots of all-inclusive family hotels, still warm in October.
2. Larnaca / Protaras / Ayia Napa, Cyprus: best "Mediterranean but still warm" option, beaches are easy with small kids, direct flights are very frequent.
3. Hurghada / Makadi Bay / Sahl Hasheesh / Marsa Alam, Egypt: warmest water and strongest sun guarantee, very good all-inclusive value, best for snorkeling.
4. Tenerife / Gran Canaria / Lanzarote, Canary Islands: reliable October climate and EU/Schengen simplicity, but longer flight and Atlantic water is cooler.
5. Crete or Rhodes, Greece: lovely if going early October, but less weather-proof for a 10-15 day beach-first trip in the second half of the holiday.

Wildcards:

- Dubai / Ras Al Khaimah: warm and easy direct flights, but expensive and very built-up.
- Cape Verde: direct leisure option and very beachy, but longer flight, windier, and less flexible with young kids.
- Mallorca: cheap and easy, but too risky for "warm/swimmable" in October unless expectations are modest.

## Planning budget guide

These are budgeting bands, not live fare quotes. Live checkout prices should be checked once families agree on 2-3 finalists.

Assumptions:

- Return flights per paid seat from Zurich, direct, school-holiday October.
- Family hotel budget assumes 2 adults + 2 kids in one family room, suite, or apartment.
- Per-family total = flights for 3-4 paid seats plus 10 nights lodging. Food basis varies by destination.
- Group total should roughly multiply by 3, but the family with the baby may be cheaper if the infant is on lap.

For the whole group, a realistic target budget range:

- Premium resort target: CHF 30,000+ total group cost, especially Cyprus premium, Dubai, or top Canaries resorts.

## Option 1: Antalya, Belek, Side, or Lara, Turkey

Why it works:

- Flight time from Zurich is about 2h55.
- Edelweiss shows 8-9 Zurich-Antalya flights/week in October 2026 and October daytime temperature around 26 deg C.
- Best value for all-inclusive resorts with pools, kids clubs, family rooms, slides, beach access, and evening entertainment.
- Easy for 3 families because large resorts can place rooms nearby and handle group transfers.

Best areas:

- Belek: polished family resorts, sandy beaches, golf resorts, close to The Land of Legends.
- Side / Kizilagac / Manavgat: strong value, nice beaches, ruins and old town nearby.
- Lara: shortest transfer from Antalya Airport, big resort hotels, less charming but very convenient.

Hotel ideas:

- TUI BLUE Palm Garden, Side/Kizilagac: family bungalows, greenery, kids club, beach, relaxed feel.
- Paloma Finesse, Side: modern family resort, good pools and slides, premium all-inclusive.
- Barut Hemera, Side: reliable higher-end family all-inclusive, beach, kids club, good food reputation.
- IC Hotels Santai Family Resort, Belek: classic family resort with kids facilities.
- TUI MAGIC LIFE Masmavi, Belek: active all-inclusive club style, good for bigger kids.
- The Land of Legends Kingdom Hotel or Nickelodeon Hotels & Resorts, Belek: maximum kid excitement; consider 2-3 nights only, then calmer beach resort.

Things to do:

- The Land of Legends theme/water park for the 6-7 year olds.
- Side old town and Apollo Temple at sunset.
- Antalya Kaleici old town and Duden waterfalls.
- Boat day from Side or Manavgat.
- Resort pools and beach days as the default rhythm.

Risks:

- Some resorts feel huge and entertainment-heavy. Choose carefully if the group wants quiet.
- Sea is good in October, but pools may be unheated unless stated.
- Check kids club minimum ages; many supervised clubs start at 4.

Verdict: Best first place to price. Probably the strongest combination of direct flight, warmth, hotel value, and kid logistics.

## Option 2: Cyprus, especially Protaras or Ayia Napa

Why it works:

- Flight time to Larnaca is about 3h30-3h45.
- Edelweiss shows 14 Zurich-Larnaca flights/week in October 2026 and October daytime temperature around 28 deg C.
- Sea temperature around Cyprus is commonly around 24-25 deg C in October.
- Protaras and Ayia Napa have sandy, clear-water beaches that are easy with children.

Best areas:

- Protaras: best family beach base, calmer evenings, Fig Tree Bay, easy restaurants.
- Ayia Napa/Nissi Beach: excellent beaches and water park nearby, busier but fine in October.
- Larnaca: easiest transfers and city convenience, but beach holiday feel is weaker.
- Paphos: good resort base, but if flying into Larnaca transfer is longer.

Hotel ideas:

- Nissi Beach Resort, Ayia Napa: beach-first, classic, lots of families.
- Olympic Lagoon Resort Ayia Napa: strong all-inclusive family resort with kids facilities.
- Louis St. Elias Resort & Waterpark, Protaras: apartment-style resort with waterpark, useful for families.
- Capo Bay Hotel, Protaras: right near Fig Tree Bay, more hotel than mega-resort.
- Grecian Park Hotel, Cape Greco/Protaras: scenic, quieter, beach shuttle/steps to Konnos Bay.
- Malama Beach Holiday Village, Protaras: apartment-style, low-key family setup.

Things to do:

- Fig Tree Bay, Konnos Bay, Nissi Beach.
- Cape Greco short walks and viewpoints.
- WaterWorld Ayia Napa if still operating on the travel dates.
- Larnaca promenade and salt lake.
- Easy boat trip from Protaras or Ayia Napa.

Risks:

- Premium family rooms can price up fast.
- Some beach areas have nightlife reputations in summer; October is calmer, but choose Protaras if in doubt.
- AI value is usually weaker than Turkey/Egypt.

Verdict: Best "warm Mediterranean without going to Egypt/Turkey" choice.

## Option 3: Egypt Red Sea, especially Hurghada, Makadi Bay, Sahl Hasheesh, Marsa Alam, or Sharm El Sheikh

Why it works:

- Hurghada flight time from Zurich is about 4h00.
- Edelweiss shows 11 Zurich-Hurghada flights/week in October 2026 and October daytime temperature around 30 deg C.
- Marsa Alam and Sharm also have October direct service from Zurich, but fewer flights.
- Red Sea water is around 26 deg C in October and snorkeling is the big highlight.
- Often the best price-to-weather ratio.

Best areas:

- Makadi Bay: good family resort cluster south of Hurghada, calmer than central Hurghada.
- Sahl Hasheesh: polished resorts, nice promenade feel.
- El Gouna: more walkable town/resort-lagoon setup, often pricier.
- Marsa Alam: best nature/reef feel, quieter, longer transfer depending on hotel.
- Sharm El Sheikh: good resort infrastructure and snorkeling, a bit more "resort bubble."

Hotel ideas:

- Jaz Aquaviva, Makadi Bay: strong family waterpark setup.
- Steigenberger Aqua Magic, Hurghada: water slides, family focus, easy airport transfer.
- Pickalbatros Jungle Aqua Park, Hurghada: maximum slides and kid energy.
- Jaz Makadi Saraya / Jaz Makadina, Makadi Bay: more balanced family resort feel.
- Baron Palace Sahl Hasheesh: higher-end option with good beach setting.
- Sunrise Arabian Beach Resort, Sharm: polished resort with beach/snorkeling access.

Things to do:

- Snorkeling boat to Giftun Islands / Orange Bay from Hurghada.
- Glass-bottom boat or semi-submarine for toddlers/babies.
- Desert sunset safari, preferably short/private with kids.
- Easy reef snorkeling from resort jetties where available.
- Luxor day trip only if adults really want it; it is long and hard with babies.

Risks:

- Need to be comfortable with a resort-centered holiday.
- Food quality varies a lot by hotel; pick carefully.
- Check reef/beach entry. Some Red Sea hotels have coral shelves and need jetty entry, not ideal for toddlers.
- Avoid hotels where the beach is mostly lagoon/artificial if snorkeling is the dream.

Verdict: Best for guaranteed sun and warm water. Choose a resort with toddler-friendly sandy beach plus reef access nearby.

## Option 4: Canary Islands, especially Tenerife, Gran Canaria, or Lanzarote

Why it works:

- Zurich direct flight is about 4h25-4h40 depending on island.
- Edelweiss shows October 2026 direct service: Tenerife 9 flights/week, Gran Canaria 8-9 flights/week.
- October daytime temperature is around 26 deg C.
- EU/Schengen logistics are simple and healthcare standards feel familiar.

Best areas:

- Tenerife South / Costa Adeje: biggest resort choice, Siam Park, good infrastructure.
- Gran Canaria / Maspalomas or Meloneras: dunes, beaches, many family resorts.
- Lanzarote / Playa Blanca or Costa Teguise: gentler scale, attractive volcanic landscape.

Hotel ideas:

- GF Victoria, Costa Adeje, Tenerife: premium family resort, suites, splash areas.
- Bahia del Duque, Costa Adeje, Tenerife: high-end beach resort, beautiful but pricey.
- H10 Suites Lanzarote Gardens, Costa Teguise: practical suite setup, family-friendly.
- Princesa Yaiza, Playa Blanca, Lanzarote: premium family resort with good location.
- Lopesan Baobab Resort, Meloneras, Gran Canaria: large family resort with strong pools.
- Seaside Los Jameos, Lanzarote: classic resort feel, usually better value than the top luxury picks.

Things to do:

- Beach and pools, but assume more pool time than sea time.
- Siam Park on Tenerife for older kids, with adult rotation for babies.
- Mount Teide cable car/drive viewpoints if staying Tenerife.
- Maspalomas dunes on Gran Canaria.
- Timanfaya National Park if staying Lanzarote.

Risks:

- Atlantic sea is cooler than Cyprus/Turkey/Egypt, around 23-24 deg C in October.
- Longer flight than eastern Mediterranean.
- Resorts can be expensive during school holidays.

Verdict: Best "safe and easy" climate choice, but not the warmest water.

## Option 5: Crete or Rhodes, Greece

Why it works:

- Direct flights from Zurich are short: Heraklion about 2h55, Rhodes about 2h40.
- Edelweiss shows strong October service: Heraklion 17 flights/week, Rhodes 6-7 flights/week.
- Early October can still be very pleasant, with sea temps around 23-24 deg C.
- Better food/culture balance than pure resort destinations.

Best areas:

- Crete: Agios Nikolaos/Elounda for calmer premium resorts; Rethymno/Georgioupolis for broader beach bases; Hersonissos for big resorts.
- Rhodes: east coast around Faliraki/Kalithea/Kolymbia/Lindos-side for calmer water and family beaches.

Hotel ideas:

- Candia Park Village, Crete: apartment-village format, very good for families.
- Creta Maris Resort, Crete: large all-inclusive, waterpark, near Hersonissos.
- Pilot Beach Resort, Crete: beach resort near Georgioupolis, spacious feel.
- The Royal Senses Resort, Crete: premium resort with family facilities and waterpark.
- Atlantica Aegean Blue, Rhodes: family resort with waterpark-style facilities.
- Amada Colossos Resort, Rhodes: large family-friendly beach resort.

Things to do:

- Crete: Knossos, Cretaquarium, Rethymno old town, short beach hops.
- Rhodes: Lindos, Rhodes Old Town, Tsambika Beach, short boat trips.
- Taverna lunches and low-key village visits.

Risks:

- Mid/late October is less reliable for a beach-first trip.
- Some resorts wind down facilities late in the season; check kids club and waterpark closing dates.
- Pools may be cool unless heated.

Verdict: Great if travel is first half of the break and the group wants culture plus beach. Not the safest for "warm and swimmable" compared with Cyprus/Turkey/Egypt.

## Backup airports

Basel:

- Worth checking for easyJet price drops.
- Useful destinations to compare: Antalya, Hurghada, Marsa Alam, Sharm El Sheikh, Larnaca/Paphos, Tenerife, Gran Canaria, Lanzarote, Malaga, Palma.
- Tradeoff: Zurich to Basel airport adds time, but can save money for 10+ paid seats.

Milan Malpensa:

- Worth checking only if the savings are large.
- Strong route network for Egypt, Canaries, Cyprus, Dubai, Maldives, Mauritius, and Mediterranean leisure routes.
- Tradeoff: road/rail transfer, parking, luggage logistics, and Italian airport stress with babies. For this group, Milan needs to save at least CHF 150-250 per paid seat to be worth it.

## Group logistics checklist

- Ask hotels for a group quote for 3 family rooms/suites, preferably adjacent or same building.
- Ask explicitly about: baby cots, high chairs, stroller accessibility, kids club ages, heated pools, splash pool hours, beach entry, and room layouts.
- For transfers, book a private minibus with 2 baby seats and 1 child booster if possible. Do not rely on taxis to have seats.
- For flight searches, compare 10 paid seats at once and also 2-3 smaller bookings. Large-party searches can sometimes price into higher fare buckets.
- Decide if the 1-year-old and baby will be lap infants or need seats. Seat choice changes both safety and cost.
- Prefer direct morning/midday flights over cheap late-night flights. With three families and babies, arrival time matters.
- For all-inclusive resorts, read recent food and kids-club reviews from autumn 2025/2026, not only summer reviews.
- Check cancellation terms carefully; three families need flexibility.
